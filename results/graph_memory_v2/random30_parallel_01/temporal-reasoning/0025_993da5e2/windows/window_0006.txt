2.3.3 Previous Studies on the Relationship between L2 Motivational Self-System, Self-Efficacy and English Achievements
In previous studies, there are abundant studies on the correlation between L2MSS and English achievement or self-efficacy and English achievement, but studies that simultaneously explore the relationship between these three variables of L2MSS, self-efficacy and English learning achievement have not yet been found in studies abroad, and only a few have been found in domestic studies, with almost all of them being master's theses (Zhao Wei, 2021; Zhou Huijuan, 2021; Chen Weilin, 2022).
Huo and Rui (2020) investigated the interactions between self-efficacy, L2MSS and English academic proficiency through a questionnaire survey of 239 junior and senior non-English majors in a university in North China. It was found that ideal L2 self and L2 learning experience had a mediating effect between self-efficacy and English proficiency, but self-efficacy could not have a significant effect on English proficiency through ought-to L2 self. The research participants of this study have a wide distribution of majors, involving science, engineering, and liberal arts, and thus are representative. In addition, in a small number of master's theses, all of which were conducted with high school students in urban areas, and questionnaires were used to explore learners' levels of L2MSS and self-efficacy, and some of the studies explored the pathways of influence among the three variables.
It is worth mentioning that the findings of these studies are not all the same. In terms of the levels of the L2MSS, Zhao (2021) and Chen (2022) agreed that the learners have a high level of the L2MSS, with the three dimensions in the order of ideal L2 self > L2 learning experience > ought-to L2 self. But Zhou (2021) found that the levels of the L2MSS are at an average level, and the L2 learning experience is the highest. The findings are not entirely consistent in terms of the correlation between the dimensions of L2MSS and English achievement. Besides, the dimensions of self-efficacy examined differed from studies.
In summary, the previous studies had the following shortcomings. Firstly, research variables are single. There are more studies on the relationship between L2MSS and English achievement or self-efficacy and English achievement, but there are very few studies on the relationship and impacts among L2MSS, self-efficacy and English achievement at the same time. Secondly, in the studies on the relationship between L2MSS, self-efficacy and English learning achievement, the research methods used are almost all quantitative analysis using questionnaires as instrument, which is unitary. Lastly, the research participants of previous studies are unitary, mainly focusing on non-English major college students and high school students in urban areas, with no attention paid to students in rural areas, thus their generalizability needs to be improved. This study selects students from rural areas as the research participants, which can increase the diversity of the sample and make this type of research more comprehensive.
Therefore, based on previous studies, this study selects high school students in a rural school in Yibin, Sichuan Province as the research participants and combines questionnaires and interview as the research instruments to investigate the correlation between self-efficacy, L2MSS and English academic achievement and their influence on learners' English academic achievement.
