# Define research goals

Defining the objective of the interview is an important step in the process, it is a crucial first step towards ensuring that the interviews are focused and effective.

Note that interviewing users should be a regular activity, preferably weekly. Because this activity is characterized by a small sample size, it is not representative, but in return allows you to discover information that cannot be obtained by large-scale studies, especially thoughts and ideas. way of thinking behind the action of the subject. So, interview regularly, thereby allowing you to regularly reach out and understand more and more about users.

However, although this is often recommended, you still need to have a good goal for each of your interviews.

### \*\*The direction of the interview should be tied to the context of the product development process\*\*

The objective of the interview should be tied to the context of the product development process. Depending on the stage of the product development process, (before, during or after development) the interview will explore different aspects of the user. Here are some examples of interview objectives based on the context of the product development process:

\*\*Before product/feature development\*\*

- Determine if the user is having a problem the product wants to solve and the severity of the problem. (or need)
- Understand how users are currently troubleshooting (or satisfying a need), if any.
- Identify the user's approach to current solutions or alternatives.
- Determine users' willingness to pay for a solution.
- Identify the factors that influence the user's decision-making process.
- ...

\*\*During product/feature development\*\*

- Check how customers perceive product ideas, features, react to messages
- Evaluate customer acceptability with product or feature, check price
- Check customer priority with specific feature requests, look for unmet requirements
- Check how customers use or react to the feature, evaluate its ease of use, ease of use
- Test hypotheses about the product or feature in general (viability, usability)

Once the product/feature is released, user interviews can be conducted to gather feedback on the user experience and identify areas for improvement.

- Understand how users are using the product/feature in their daily life
- Measure user satisfaction and identify areas for improvement
- Explain why the user left or why the user stayed
- Collect feedback on potential improvements or new features for future iterations
- Measure product/feature success based on user adoption and usage
- Collect testimonials or success stories for marketing purposes.

### \*\*Interview result must be actionable\*\*

In many cases, interview results are forgotten. The team may have conducted many interviews, but the results of these interviews did not add significant value to the work of the product development team.

Interview questions should help identify problems, user needs, or test hypotheses about the product under development. Interview questions should be designed in such a way as to identify information relevant to decision-making for the product. The results of the interviews should be used to re-evaluate the strategy and direction of product development. And it takes coordination between departments in the company to interview users effectively.

There's a saying I use to remind myself: "Before you ask a question, you need to know what you're going to do with the answer."
