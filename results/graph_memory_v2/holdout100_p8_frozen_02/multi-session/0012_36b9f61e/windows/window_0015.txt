Genre: Fiction, Fantasy

A fire roared in the fireplace of the Queen’s personal library. Roran was curled up on the floor with a blanket and pillow as his mother read him fairytales. Her voice was soft and warm, like the fire, and it wrapped around Roran like a hug. He loved evenings like this. He loved listening to his mother tell stories and the scratching of his father’s quill. He often joined them for stories and the three spent most of their evenings in this room before Roran was sent to bed.
Roran was the only child of King Daris and Queen Jaelin. He knew they wished to give him siblings, but for reasons he didn’t understand, they wouldn’t. So they showered him in as much love as they had to give. And he was happy. Even at the young age of four years old, he knew how much they loved him and he loved them. Even though they were King and Queen, they spent as much time as they could with him, including times like this.
But this evening took a surprising turn. The door to the library burst open and Mage Kevala was standing in the doorway, a shocked look on her face.
“Your Majesties!”
“Kevala? What is the meaning of disrupting us at this hour?” King Daris stood from the small desk where he had been writing, his voice angry.
Roran sat up. Kevala looked like she was about to cry and it struck Roran as odd. She had always been so strict and controlled. It was weird to see her emotions so open. Roran, suddenly scared, ran over to his mother and she drew him up into her lap, comforting him with a hug.
“It’s Greschel.”
The King’s anger disappeared, “Greschel? What’s going on?”
Kevala looked back and forth between the King and Queen and she revealed a folded parchment. Roran pulled away a little from his mother’s embrace, his curiosity winning out over his fear. The King took the parchment, and even in the dim light of the fire, Roran could see the colour drain from his face.
“Greschel is… dead!”
“What?” The Queen’s arms loosened around Roran and her mouth had dropped open.
Roran’s father turned around, his face blank with grief, “He had decided to travel north alone, before his company was ready to travel with him. He got sick and died before the healer could get there. I don’t understand. How could this happen?”
The room went quiet. Greschel had been the High Mage of Ascai and advisor to their majesties. Roran didn’t really know Greschel. The few times he had met the man, it had been brief, rather scary encounters. Greschel was tall and wore a scowl like his father wore a smile; all the time. Roran hadn’t liked him too much and always felt that Greschel had been watching him, disappointment and displeasure surrounding him like a cloud.
But Roran knew that his parents had liked him and their grief filled the room.
Roran looked at Kevala. She looked back at him, fear in her eyes. As the King walked back over to the Queen, Kevala raised a hand like she was going to say something, but seemed to decide not to and stayed quiet. Her eyes lingered on Roran and he shifted uncomfortably in his mother’s lap, her frightened gaze igniting a new fear in Roran, though he couldn’t understand why. He turned back to his mother, who now had silent tears running down her face. He leaned into her and let her wrap her arms around him as she cried.
