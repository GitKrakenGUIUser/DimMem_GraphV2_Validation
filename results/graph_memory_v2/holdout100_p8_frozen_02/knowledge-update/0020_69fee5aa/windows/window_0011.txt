The role of user research in product development

- Explain user behavior
- Gain understanding of user needs and preferences
- Identify user pain, difficulty, barriers
- Understand how customers feel about a solution, product, feature, etc

From there, businesses and product development teams can:

- Select target customer segments
- User's choice of problem to solve
- Provide appropriate pricing strategies, reach channels, messages, etc
- Make a selection of features to develop and UI/UX

For example

Research helps businesses realize there is a feature that loyal users regularly use, but most new users do not notice. From there, businesses can change the priority of that feature in the onboard experience for new users
- User research helps explain why customers choose the company's product, over the competition. From there, the company understands the strengths that it needs to maintain, and at the same time chooses the target market to continue exploiting.
User research helps explain why customers don't use a particular feature, either because the feature doesn't meet their needs or because of an unfriendly design.

\*\*The role of interviewing in the user research process.\*\*

In user research, user interviews are important because

- Helps build a more realistic image of the customer in the product development team's mind, avoiding the fantasy of being far from reality.
- Provide visual explanations for user behavior
- Allows to feel the user's emotions and views
- Allows to dig into the context, logic of user actions.
- Get instant feedback on the product development team's assumptions
